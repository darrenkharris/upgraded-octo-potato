# SEO Plan for a UK ICT Consultancy

## Primary positioning

Clear, security-conscious ICT consultancy and IT support for home users, organisations, schools and charities across the UK.

## Priority audiences and search intent

1. Home users: need trusted, patient help with everyday technology problems.
2. Small businesses and organisations: need reliable IT support, cyber security and cloud guidance.
3. Schools and education settings: need strategic ICT support, cyber resilience and DfE standards readiness.
4. Charities and community groups: need affordable, proportionate IT help and safer systems.

## Local SEO plan

Replace UK-wide generic copy with specific service areas once confirmed. Create one strong local page per meaningful area only. Avoid thin duplicated pages.

Example pages:

- /it-support-[town-or-region]/
- /school-ict-support-[county]/
- /charity-it-support-[city]/

Include genuine local signals: service area, travel area, local examples, local business profile, consistent name/address/phone if publishing an office address, and region-specific FAQs.

## Conversion plan

Primary CTA: Request a free ICT health check.
Secondary CTA: Find support for your audience.
Trust builders to add later: named experience, certifications, partner badges, anonymised case studies, testimonials, clear pricing ranges, response times, and professional memberships.

## Technical SEO checklist

- Replace example.co.uk in canonical URL, sitemap and robots.txt.
- Add a real favicon and social sharing image.
- Keep one clear H1 on each page.
- Use descriptive internal links.
- Compress images before upload.
- Add Google Search Console and submit sitemap.
- Avoid unnecessary third-party scripts.
- Test mobile performance with Lighthouse or PageSpeed Insights.

## Accessibility checklist

- Keep visible labels on all forms.
- Maintain strong colour contrast.
- Keep keyboard focus styles visible.
- Avoid hover-only interactions.
- Use meaningful alt text for content images.
- Keep headings in logical order.
- Publish an accessibility statement if required by your organisation or sector.

## Security and privacy checklist

- Use HTTPS.
- Enable MFA for GitHub, Cloudflare and domain registrar accounts.
- Do not commit passwords, API keys, tokens or private client data.
- Replace the demo contact form with a trusted form provider or secure backend before collecting enquiries through the website.
- Update privacy notice when analytics, forms or embedded services are added.
- Use Cloudflare security headers or equivalent hosting configuration.
